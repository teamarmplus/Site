# SiteVerdict — Competitor Understanding & Positioning

**Purpose:** Understand the market clearly. Learn from it. Do not copy it.

> "We do not compete by being louder. We compete by being more useful, more transparent, more verified, more connected, and more trusted."

*Last updated: May 2026. Unverified claims are marked (NV). These notes are for internal strategic understanding only — not for public use.*

---

## How to read this document

For each competitor or category:
- What they appear to do well (from public observation)
- Who they appear to serve
- What SiteVerdict can learn from them
- What SiteVerdict should NOT copy
- How SiteVerdict can compete honestly

---

## 1. Archistar

**What they appear to do well (NV):**
- Combines zoning, planning controls, design envelopes and building feasibility in one platform
- Offers 3D massing and yield visualisation
- Serves real estate agents, developers and investors
- Has significant data partnerships and national coverage

**Who they serve (NV):**
- Developers, real estate agents, investors, town planners, architects

**What SiteVerdict can learn:**
- The combination of planning controls + feasibility in one view is powerful for decision-makers
- Visual output (envelope, yield) makes abstract planning data tangible
- Serving professionals with data-rich tools creates recurring usage

**What SiteVerdict should NOT copy:**
- Subscription pricing that excludes small investors and homeowners
- Complexity that requires training to use
- Becoming a sales tool disguised as intelligence

**How SiteVerdict can compete:**
- Free entry point with genuine usefulness for everyday users, not just professionals
- Trust-first positioning: data sources shown, uncertainty acknowledged
- Human connection layer (services, professionals, finance) that Archistar doesn't offer at ground level

## 2. Landchecker

**What they appear to do well (NV):**
- Combines cadastral data, planning layers, heritage and zoning in a clean map interface
- Strong NSW and Victorian coverage
- Used by real estate and development professionals

**Who they serve (NV):**
- Real estate agents, buyers, developers, planners

**What SiteVerdict can learn:**
- Clean map + layers interface reduces friction for non-technical users
- Combining multiple government layers into one search is valuable

**What SiteVerdict should NOT copy:**
- Map-only UX without a clear decision-support output
- Competing on data breadth alone without depth of interpretation

**How SiteVerdict can compete:**
- SiteVerdict interprets data into a verdict, not just a display
- SiteVerdict connects data to action (services, professionals, finance) — Landchecker does not
- SiteVerdict's free tier creates trust before a paywall

---

## 3. ZoneDSS / NSW Zone Tool (NSW Government)

**What they do well:**
- Official NSW zoning and planning data
- Free to the public
- Directly authoritative — zone data comes from the source

**Who they serve:**
- Anyone checking NSW zoning — free and official

**What SiteVerdict can learn:**
- Free access to ground-truth zoning data matters
- Users trust data that comes directly from government sources
- The raw tool is under-interpreted — there is space for layered intelligence on top

**What SiteVerdict should NOT copy:**
- Presenting raw government data without context
- Making users do the interpretation themselves

**How SiteVerdict can compete:**
- SiteVerdict wraps government data (zone, overlays, DA timeline) in an intelligible verdict
- SiteVerdict adds the professional and service connection layer that government tools cannot
- SiteVerdict is honest that its source is government data — this builds, not hides, trust

## 7. DA Leads

**What they do well:**
- Commercial API for NSW residential subdivision DA data
- Real-time approved DA records with address, cost, lot count, coordinates
- Serves proptech developers and data companies

**Who they serve:**
- Software developers, proptech platforms, property data companies

**What SiteVerdict can learn:**
- Real approved DA data is the ground truth for subdivision intelligence
- API-based data access is scalable
- Comparable DAs are highly valuable context for a site check

**What SiteVerdict should NOT copy:**
- Presenting DA data as buy signals without context
- Using DA data for speculative recommendation

**How SiteVerdict uses DA Leads:**
- Used as one input in comparable DA analysis (not the sole source)
- Never presented as investment recommendations
- API key kept server-side, never exposed

## 8. NSW Planning Portal / ePlanning (Government)

**What they do well:**
- Official authoritative source for LEP zones, overlays, lot size, heritage, flood, bushfire
- Free public access
- ePlanning API provides machine-readable planning controls
- 34-council subdivision DA dataset (used for median timeline calculations)

**What SiteVerdict can learn:**
- The raw data is available — the gap is interpretation and accessibility
- Government tools are authoritative but inaccessible to everyday users
- DA median data from ePlanning is a genuine differentiator

**What SiteVerdict does with NSW Planning Portal:**
- Queries 16+ planning layers live per Site Check
- Uses the ePlanning DA dataset for council median timelines
- Shows data sources in the report — never hides the origin

## 9. General property portals (Domain, REA, PropTrack, CoreLogic)

**What they do well:**
- Transaction data, price history, comparable sales
- National coverage
- Consumer trust built over decades

**What SiteVerdict should NOT copy:**
- Focusing on price/value rather than planning intelligence
- Becoming a general real estate portal

**How SiteVerdict differs:**
- SiteVerdict focuses on planning risk and development potential, not price
- Planning intelligence is underserved in the consumer/investor market
- SiteVerdict builds toward professional users, not mass consumer

## SiteVerdict Positioning Summary

| Dimension | Competitor approach | SiteVerdict approach |
|---|---|---|
| Data | Proprietary aggregation | Open government data, transparently sourced |
| Price | Subscription / pro pricing | Free entry, paid full report |
| Output | Map / visual | Verdict + action pathway |
| Advice | Sometimes implicit | Explicit: no advice, verified sources only |
| User | Professionals | Everyone, professionals included |
| Trust | Brand trust | Data transparency + professional CTA |
| Action | View data | Connect to services, professionals, finance |

---

## What we must never do

- Present planning intelligence as investment advice
- Use "Strong Buy", "guaranteed return", "bank-grade analysis"
- Claim accuracy we cannot verify
- Use competitor data or branding without permission
- Build features faster than we can maintain trust in them
